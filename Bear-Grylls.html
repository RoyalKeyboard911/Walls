London
Exploration

Introduction

London offers students an exciting dive into one of the world’s most vibrant theatre scenes. As they explore the city, they’ll attend a variety of live performances—from timeless Shakespearean dramas at the Globe Theatre to modern productions in the West End. Beyond just watching, students will engage with the cultural and historical context of each play, gaining a deeper appreciation for the art of storytelling. Walking through the same streets once traveled by literary greats, they’ll experience the magic of London as both a classroom and a stage.

Schedule

During their time in London, students will begin each day with guided city tours, exploring iconic landmarks like the Tower of London, Buckingham Palace, and the British Museum. Afternoons will be spent visiting educational sites such as the Science Museum and the Tate Modern, with time set aside for group discussions and reflection. Evenings are reserved for attending live theatre performances, offering a mix of classic and contemporary plays. There will also be free time for students to explore local markets, enjoy traditional cuisine, and experience the city's vibrant atmosphere.
